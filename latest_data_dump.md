# Black Sea Monitor — Digest 2026-06-13

## Civilian Conditions (Crimea/Azov fuel, food, rationing)
Crimea is experiencing its worst fuel crisis since Russia's full occupation began. Multiple sources (AP/ABC News 2026-06-12, RFE/RL Crimea.Realities 2026-06-08, Military.com 2026-06-12, OilPrice 2026-06-12) report gasoline shortages spreading across the peninsula, with motorists waiting in long lines at filling stations. A vacationer in Yalta posted that she and her husband are stranded with only enough fuel to drive 50 km, while the nearest station with supply is 80 km away in Simferopol (RFE/RL 2026-06-06). Russian regional governors are publicly denying a fuel crisis — a pattern typically indicating one exists (OilPrice 2026-06-12). Train service to Crimea was suspended after a Ukrainian drone disabled a locomotive, further squeezing civilian movement (RFE/RL 2026-06-08).

## Infrastructure & Logistics (strikes, corridor, ports)
Ukraine has intensified mid-range strikes on the R-280 "Novorossiya" highway — Russia's primary land supply corridor to Crimea. Multiple outlets describe tanker trucks attacked and left ablaze along this route (AP 2026-06-12, Daily Mail 2026-06-12, Business Insider 2026-06-03). The Chonhar bridge connecting mainland Ukraine to northern Crimea was reportedly struck twice ("double-tapped") by Ukrainian drones (Fark aggregation 2026-06-11). Ukraine's 412th Nemesis Brigade claims a new mid-range drone called the "Morrigan" is being used to hit logistics targets 60 miles behind lines (Business Insider 2026-06-03). Separately, Ukraine struck five cargo vessels in Mariupol and Berdyansk ports overnight June 4–5, killing five Azerbaijani nationals (GlobalSecurity/PressTV 2026-06-05, BBC 2026-06-05).

The sustained campaign against refineries, fuel depots, and transport corridors is described as causing "significant damage" to Russian oil infrastructure capacity (multiple sources).
