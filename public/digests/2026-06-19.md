# Black Sea Monitor — Digest 2026-06-19

## Civilian Conditions (Crimea/Azov fuel, food, rationing)

Sevastopol fuel rationing remains at 20 liters per week with QR-code purchasing; ISW's June 18 Occupation Update confirms approximately 80% of Crimea's June tourist bookings cancelled, threatening up to 18% of the peninsula's annual revenue. Moscow Times analysis (June 18) notes that while the national-level fuel crisis is manageable in the short term, Crimea and frontline regions face the most acute shortages due to logistics disruption compounding refinery attrition. Russian-installed Crimea governor banned motorcycles and scooters 8pm–6am, citing drone-mimicking noise interference with air defenses (MSN/The Independent, June 18). ATESH partisan networks report occupation officials and FSB personnel evacuating families from Crimea, many targeting completion before June 21 [unverified - Ukrainian partisan source] (UNN, June 19).

## Infrastructure & Logistics (strikes, corridor, ports)

Ukraine struck the Moscow Kapotnya refinery for the second time in one week on June 18, launching 555 drones overnight and halting all four Moscow airports; ISW (June 18) confirms fires at five refinery locations, including the combined oil refining unit and oil tank farm. The Gukovo oil depot in Rostov Oblast was also struck (ISW, June 18). Ukrainian drone units struck the rail bridge near Rozdolne over the North Crimean Canal and multiple road bridges linking Crimea to occupied Kherson, with Cryptobriefing (June 18) reporting traffic on the R-280 Novorossiya highway down 66–71% since mid-June. Reuters (June 18) confirms the rebuilt Azov Corps struck Mariupol port's electrical substations, repair facilities, and a sanctioned vessel in a June 10 operation, with Ukrainian drones described as having established "permanent fire control" on highways around Mariupol. ATESH reports Russian Black Sea Fleet command is developing plans to relocate remaining Sevastopol facilities to Novorossiysk [unverified - Ukrainian partisan source] (CTR Center, June 17).

## Real Estate & Property Administration

ISW's June 18 Occupation Update reports Putin signed a law lowering the Free Economic Zone minimum first-year capital investment threshold from 30% to 10% across occupied Donetsk, Luhansk, Zaporizhia, and Kherson oblasts, explicitly because the FEZ has failed to attract expected buy-in. Putin held a June 13 video conference on occupied territory development, claiming nearly one million square meters of housing commissioned since 2023 [unverified - Russian state source]. CEPA (June 18) documents Chinese equipment sales and quarry operations in occupied Donetsk, including the Karansky Quarry employing 243 workers with Chinese machinery; analysts caution this is equipment supply rather than durable investment, and Beijing is unlikely to formally operate in territories of uncertain legal status.

## Refinery & Export Pressure

Energy Intelligence estimates approximately one-third of Russia's oil refining capacity has been knocked out by Ukrainian drone strikes before the June 18 Moscow attack (RFE/RL, June 18); ISW (June 18) adds that Russia is now importing gasoline from Asian countries by sea for the first time, having previously managed domestic supply in 2025 without imports — indicating 2026 shortages are materially worse. Russia's fuel subsidy policy cost approximately 2.6 trillion rubles ($35.4 billion) in 2025, equivalent to roughly half the federal budget deficit (Moscow Times, June 18). Ukrainian General Staff confirms June 14 strikes at the Palkino Oil Pumping Station in Yaroslavl Oblast destroyed seven tanks totaling 95,000 cubic meters (ISW, June 18).

## Banking & Mortgage Programs

Sberbank now operates 30 offices, 332 ATMs, and 16,000 payment terminals across occupied Ukraine; its occupied Luhansk branch has surpassed one million customers and opened 17 locations since June 2024, with the SberBlizhe loyalty program enrolling over 140,000 Luhansk Oblast residents (ISW Occupation Update, June 18). The FEZ investment barrier reduction and Sberbank expansion are parallel instruments of Russian financial integration pressure; no new mortgage subsidy or developer data was reported in this period.
