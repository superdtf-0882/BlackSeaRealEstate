## July 10, 2026

### Civilian Conditions (Crimea/Azov fuel, food, rationing)

Ukraine's multi-day tanker campaign has directly worsened Crimea's fuel access, with Sevastopol receiving only a third of daily fuel needs on average according to occupation governor Razvozhayev (Kyiv Independent, July 9). Russian statistical data cited by Mezha.net shows Crimea fuel prices rose 78.4% in a single week as of July 8. Putin acknowledged the crisis and called for emergency financial subsidies for Crimea residents on July 8, while ISW's occupation update (July 9) documents concurrent food price spikes and mobile communications failures in Feodosia due to power outages. These developments extend and deepen conditions already logged in key_events through July 8.

### Infrastructure & Logistics (strikes, corridor, ports)

Ukraine's Unmanned Systems Forces struck at least 36 Russian vessels — primarily shadow fleet tankers — in the Sea of Azov and Black Sea between July 6 and July 9 (BBC, July 9; Mezha.net, July 9). The campaign specifically targeted tankers on the Taganrog-to-Crimea fuel route, with two tankers each reportedly carrying ~7,000 tons of fuel confirmed destroyed. Separately, Ukrainian drones struck the Ilsky refinery in Krasnodar Krai on the night of July 9–10 — a facility serving as a distribution hub via Novorossiysk and Taman (Censor.NET, July 10). The Moynaki substation in Yevpatoria was struck, cutting power to the city (Mezha.net, July 10). ISW assessed the 35+ tanker strikes as "a new phase" in Ukraine's Crimea isolation campaign, combining land corridor degradation with maritime interdiction simultaneously; Russia cannot effectively counter it after the Crimean Defense Group was disbanded in 2024 (ISW, July 9).

### Real Estate & Property Administration

ISW's July 9 occupation update confirmed occupation officials in Luhansk Oblast published a June 16 decree simplifying the process for designating properties as "ownerless," enabling broader seizure and redistribution. Rosreestr identified 1.9 million properties missing from the state register and 63,000 unused land parcels across occupied oblasts. In Zaporizhzhia Oblast, 618 residential properties were transferred to law enforcement, healthcare, and education personnel as of July 4. In Mariupol, the first apartment renovated under the municipal co-financing program — seized after designation as ownerless — was transferred to a new owner on July 3 (ISW, July 9). These moves are consistent with the previously logged demographic replacement strategy and do not represent new program initiation.

### Refinery & Export Pressure

Ukrainian drone strikes on July 8–9 hit oil depots in Stavropol Krai (585 km from front), Tver Oblast (520 km), and the Yug Rusi oil loading terminal in Bataysk, Rostov Oblast (215 km), with governors confirming fires at all three (ISW, July 9). The Saratov refinery halted processing on July 8 following a July 7 strike (Reuters, July 9). On the night of July 9–10, the Ilsky refinery in Krasnodar Krai was struck — a 3 million ton/year facility supplying the Southern Federal District and exporting via Novorossiysk and Taman (Censor.NET, July 10). The Financial Times reported Ukrainian strikes have disabled up to 40% of Russia's oil refining capacity as of July 9. A Chevron-chartered Turkish tanker, Yasa Polaris, was struck near the CPC terminal outside Novorossiysk on July 7 (The Insider, July 9). ATESH reported Russian Interior Ministry cadets are being deployed to patrol refineries and depots [unverified — Ukrainian partisan source].

### Banking & Mortgage Programs

No significant developments.

### Dashboard Relevance

- **RPI:** Ilsky refinery strike (July 10, Krasnodar Krai) adds a new confirmed facility directly supplying Black Sea port exports. RPI at or near ceiling; maritime corridor now functionally closed for fuel delivery to Crimea. The dual-vector blockade (land + maritime) is qualitatively new and not yet captured as a key_event. Suggested key_event: "July 7–9: Ukraine initiates maritime phase of Crimea isolation campaign — 36 shadow fleet tankers struck in 96 hours, ISW confirms Russia lacks naval capacity to defend sea routes."
- **CCI:** 78.4% single-week fuel price increase and one-third daily fuel supply confirmation (Razvozhayev) are new quantitative data points. CCI at or near ceiling; Ilsky strike extends pressure geographically into the primary alternative supply region for Crimea.
- **Track B (Crimea, value: 30):** Moynaki substation strike cutting power to Yevpatoria is continued civic infrastructure failure. No new category of failure beyond existing floor. Monitor only.

## July 12, 2026

### Civilian Conditions (Crimea/Azov fuel, food, rationing)

Crimea authorities banned all civilian fuel sales, restricting supply exclusively to the military and essential services, while water access in some areas was limited to one hour per day (The Telegraph/UNN, July 11). A grey-market fuel economy is operating openly via Telegram and platforms including Ozon, Wildberries, and Avito, with prices reported at up to $25 per gallon (BurgasMedia/Fakti.bg, July 11). Russian Rosstat data (July 11) showed consumer gasoline prices rose 6.88% in June 2026 — nearly ten times the monthly rate seen earlier in the year — with diesel up 7.1% and annual gasoline inflation at 19.9% (ISW, July 11). A Sevastopol resident quoted by The Telegraph described shops closing or accepting only cash due to persistent power cuts.

### Infrastructure & Logistics (strikes, corridor, ports)

ISW confirmed 76 Russian vessels struck in the Sea of Azov between July 6 and July 11, including 21 tankers in a single overnight operation July 10–11, with Starboard Maritime Intelligence data showing a 55% decline in active AIS transponders in the sea over that period (ISW, July 11). Russia halted shipping through the Don-Azov Canal following the attacks (Reuters, July 10–11), effectively closing the maritime resupply route Russia had pivoted to after R-280 highway and bridge damage. Ukraine's General Staff confirmed strikes on 10 tankers and four ferries overnight July 11–12, a fuel train near occupied Tokmak in Zaporizhzhia Oblast, and updated BDA from the Novatek Ust-Luga processing complex strike (Ukrayinska Pravda, July 12). Attacks continued on Chongar and Henichesk bridges and the R-280 "Novorossiya" highway. Ukrainian strikes also reportedly destroyed a repair base of the Russian 58th Combined Arms Army at the Dormash Plant in occupied Berdyansk (ISW, July 11).

### Real Estate & Property Administration

No new real estate or property administration developments. The July 8 confirmation of 30–50% Crimea price collapse remains the operative context.

### Refinery & Export Pressure

Ukraine struck the Syzran oil refinery in Samara Oblast overnight July 11–12, setting it ablaze; capacity approximately 8.5–9 million tons/year, supplies fuel to Russian military units in central and southern Russia (Ukrainian General Staff/EA WorldView, July 12). ISW's July 11 assessment confirmed Ukraine has struck all but one of Russia's major refineries in 2026, with most hit multiple times; Russian authorities have imposed rationing in more than 50 regions with Crimea banning all civilian sales entirely. Russian authorities allocated 4.3 billion rubles (~$55.9 million) in subsidies to Crimea tourism companies (ISW, July 11).

### Banking & Mortgage Programs

No significant developments.

Don-Azov Canal closure removes the last major resupply alternative that had partially offset prior CCI drivers. Hold at ceiling; flag for structural persistence.
- **RPI:** Syzran refinery strike, Don-Azov Canal closure, and 55% AIS transponder decline in Sea of Azov represent a new structural vector (export corridor, not just refinery capacity). Maritime export corridor now functionally closed.
- **Track B (Crimea, value: 30):** Total civilian fuel ban plus one-hour daily water access represents further deterioration. Suggested nudge toward 27–28 pending next formal scoring cycle, as the civilian fuel total ban removes remaining basis for normal residential commitment.

## July 14, 2026

### Civilian Conditions (Crimea/Azov fuel, food, rationing)

Crimea civilian fuel conditions have deteriorated to an effective ban on public retail sales at most stations, with pump prices at operating outlets reaching 330–450 rubles per liter ($3.50–$5.85/liter, up to $22.14/gallon) in Yalta and Yevpatoria as of July 13 — roughly 3–5× the mainland Russian average and reportedly the most expensive retail gasoline on earth (Kyiv Post, July 13). El País reporting from Feodosia (July 13) confirms fuel entirely reserved for essential services and administration vehicles, QR-code allocation in Sevastopol, constant power cuts, food service closures from grid failures, and zero tourism — the Russian Tourism Industry Union estimates 70% of hotel bookings cancelled. ATESH reports the 1096th Anti-Aircraft Missile Regiment and mobile fire groups near Chonhar operating with near-empty fuel tanks and abandoning vehicles mid-mission [unverified — Ukrainian partisan source] (Ukrinform, July 14; United24 Media, July 13).

### Infrastructure & Logistics (strikes, corridor, ports)

Ukraine's Unmanned Systems Forces struck 105 Russian vessels in the Sea of Azov over July 6–13, including 15 on the night of July 12–13 (seven tankers, five dry-cargo ships, one ferry, two tugs); SAR imagery confirms total vessel count in the sea fell from 132 to 43 over that period (ISW, July 13; Euromaidan Press, July 13). Russia halted the Don-Azov canal and stopped accepting Kerch Strait passage requests, completing a near-total maritime blockade of Crimea's supply routes. The Kerch Bridge was closed for 11+ hours overnight July 12–13 following drone attacks on the Kerch energy point and air defenses (Euromaidan Press, July 13). A Ukrainian drone struck a vessel in occupied Mariupol port on July 13 (Censor.NET). Ukraine struck a semi-underground Russian logistics hub near Armyansk on July 13, confirmed by NASA FIRMS thermal anomalies (United24 Media, July 13). Overnight July 13–14, drone strikes hit the Afipsk oil refinery tank farm near Krasnodar and the Salavat Gazpromneftekhim complex in Bashkortostan (Liga.net, July 14; NeMoskva, July 14).

### Real Estate & Property Administration

El País ground reporting from Feodosia (July 13) provides civilian corroboration of the real estate demand collapse logged July 8: a student reports no employment and "everything revolves around tourism, but there's no people and no money," consistent with the 30–50% price decline previously recorded. No new transaction, listing, or registration data.

### Refinery & Export Pressure

Russian oil refining has fallen to its lowest level since 2005 (Kyiv Post, July 14; Mezha.net, July 14). Ukraine's SBU 40-day campaign summary (July 13) lists strikes on the Syzran refinery AVT-5 and AVT-6 units, the Cherkasy oil pumping station in Bashkortostan (Transneft-Ural, ~2M ton/year), the Yaroslavl refinery, the Vysotsk Baltic export terminal, TES-Terminal-1 fuel storage in occupied Kerch, and oil depots in Tver and Stavropol (Kyiv Post, July 13). The state procurement system for fuel is collapsing — ten canceled state fuel tenders across Samara, Tambov, and Stavropol, with ambulance and utility services running on reserves (Izvestiya via Kyiv Post, July 13). Deputy PM Novak publicly acknowledged a national fuel crisis "due to [Ukrainian] strikes" (Kyiv Post, July 13).

## July 15, 2026

### Civilian Conditions (Crimea/Azov fuel, food, rationing)

Sevastopol's Moscow-installed governor Razvozhayev announced July 14 rotating blackouts of two hours on followed by six hours off following overnight Ukrainian strikes on energy infrastructure (Reuters/Devdiscourse, July 14). The Chairperson of the Crimean Tatar Mejlis Refat Chubarov stated July 14 that many small and medium enterprises in occupied Crimea have halted operations and livestock farms are manually performing previously mechanized processes due to combined electricity and fuel failures; prolonged outages in northern Crimea are also affecting water supply (ISW, July 14). Gasoline rationing remains in force peninsula-wide under the state of emergency with civilian fuel sales suspended; prices at operating stations in Yalta approximately 350 rubles per liter.

### Infrastructure & Logistics (strikes, corridor, ports)

ATESH partisans report July 14 that Russian occupation authorities have begun removing 10–40 MVA transformers from Northern Crimean Canal pumping stations — idle since the Kakhovka dam destruction — to replace substations destroyed by Ukrainian strikes, with ATESH describing this as a "dead end" as spare stocks are exhausted [unverified — Ukrainian partisan source] (Euromaidan Press, July 14). Ukrainian drone forces claimed 116 vessels struck in the Sea of Azov over nine days as of July 14, including five tankers, five bulk carriers, and a tugboat overnight July 13–14; the Kerch Strait and Azov-Don channel remained restricted to vessel traffic (Reuters, July 14; France 24, July 14). The Ukrainian Navy sank the Russian FSB patrol vessel Izumrud near Novorossiysk using a Sargan-3000 unmanned surface vehicle on July 14 (Kyiv Independent, Euromaidan Press, July 14). Ukrainian forces also struck an ammunition depot in occupied Donetsk and a logistics hub in occupied Luhansk (Hromadske/New Voice of Ukraine, July 14).

### Real Estate & Property Administration

### Refinery & Export Pressure

Ukraine struck the Gazprom Neftekhim Salavat complex in Bashkortostan overnight July 13–14 — the last major Russian gasoline producer not previously hit in 2026 — damaging the AVT-6 primary crude oil distillation unit at a facility with 10 million ton/year capacity approximately 1,300–1,500 km from the front (ISW, Kyiv Independent, New Voice of Ukraine, July 14). Ukraine also struck the Afipsky refinery in Krasnodar Krai (6.25 million ton/year) and a ship transshipment area near Gelendzhik used for oil transfer and military naval logistics (Ukraine General Staff via Hromadske, July 14; Moscow Times, July 14). Russia's gasoline production running at approximately 65% of average seasonal consumption following cumulative drone attacks.
